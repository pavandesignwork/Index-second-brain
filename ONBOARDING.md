# Onboarding (read this when the user wants to set up their brain)

This is a one-time setup. Your job is to interview the user and then write two files: `about-company.md` and `voice-and-brand.md`. When done, both files should be filled with real answers and the `<!-- ONBOARDING:UNFILLED -->` marker removed from each.

Run this when the user says "run onboarding", "set up the brain", or agrees to your first-run offer.

## How to run it

Keep it human. You're having a short conversation, not filling in a form.

- Ask in small batches — two or three questions at a time. Wait for the answer, then continue.
- Plain language. Assume no technical background unless they tell you otherwise.
- Everything is skippable. If they say "skip" or "not sure", move on and leave that part blank.
- Don't interrogate. If an answer covers two questions, don't re-ask.
- Reflect back after each batch — briefly confirm what you heard so they can correct you.
- Aim for 5 minutes, not 30.

## What to find out

### Part 1 — About the company (fills `about-company.md`)

1. **Name and what it does.** "What's the company called, and what does it actually do — one sentence?"
2. **Who it serves.** "Who are your customers or users?"
3. **The team.** "How many people, and what are the main roles?" Rough is fine.
4. **Stage and current focus.** "Where are you right now — pre-launch, early customers, scaling? What's the main thing you're focused on this month?"
5. **What's on the plate right now.** "What are the one or two most active things the team is working on?" Tell them this part goes stale fastest and they should update it often.
6. **Tools the team uses.** "What do you live in day-to-day? Slack, Notion, GitHub — whatever's relevant."

### Part 2 — How the company communicates (fills `voice-and-brand.md`)

7. **Brand voice.** "When you write something that sounds like your company, how does it read? Short and punchy, or detailed? Formal or casual?" If unsure, ask them to share a sentence or two they've actually sent — and infer the pattern.
8. **Words to never use.** "Anything that feels off-brand? Words or phrases you'd never put in an email or post?"
9. **Internal vs external tone.** "Is the tone the same with the team as it is with customers? Or does it shift?"
10. **Visual identity.** Optional. "Do you have a logo, colour palette, or style guide?" If yes, capture the basics. If no, skip and remove that section from the file.

## When you're done

1. Write their answers into `about-company.md` and `voice-and-brand.md`, replacing all placeholder content.
2. Remove the `<!-- ONBOARDING:UNFILLED -->` line from the top of both files.
3. Show the user both finished files and ask: *"Does this sound right? Change anything that's off."*
4. Make any corrections they ask for.
5. Tell them they're set up, and that they can update these files any time just by telling you — for example: "add to about-company that we just hired a designer."

Keep the writing in their own words. The point of these files is that they sound like the company, not like generic AI output.
